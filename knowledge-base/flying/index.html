🎮 Flying Styles & Techniques

From freestyle to racing to cinematic — find your flying style

Flight Modes Explained

ModeStabilityDifficultyUse
Acro (Rate)None — full manualHardAll FPV flying. Learn this.
Angle (Level)Self-levelsEasyEmergency recovery only

Acro mode is FPV. Angle and Horizon are training wheels. The goal is to fly Acro 100% of the time. It takes 10-50 hours of practice to feel comfortable.

Freestyle

🛩️ What is Freestyle?

Freestyle is the most popular FPV style. It's about creative expression — flips, rolls, gaps, proximity flying, and smooth flow. There are no rules. You fly wherever you want, however you want.

Best Setup for Freestyle

  • Frame: 5-inch (most versatile)
  • Weight: 600-700g with battery
  • Video: DJI O4 Pro or HDZero
  • Battery: 6S 1100-1300mAh
  • Props: 5x4.3x3 (balanced pitch)
  • Tune: Medium-aggressive PIDs, some expo

Racing

🏁 What is Racing?

FPV racing involves flying through obstacle courses (gates/flags) at maximum speed. Races are typically 3-5 laps. The DRL (Drone Racing League) and MultiGP are the main organizations.

Racing Characteristics

  • Speed: 100-200+ km/h on straights
  • Course: Gates, flags, tight turns
  • Format: Timed, head-to-head, or multi-pilot
  • Video: Analog (lowest latency) or HDZero

Best Setup for Racing

  • Frame: 5-inch lightweight carbon
  • Weight: Under 500g (without battery)
  • Motors: High KV (2400-2700KV on 4S)
  • Video: Analog or HDZero (lowest latency)
  • Battery: High C-rating, 4S or 6S
  • Props: Steep pitch (5x4.8x3 or 5x5x3)
  • Tune: Aggressive PIDs, minimal expo

Racing Tips

  • Fly the line, not the gates — look ahead
  • Smooth throttle control > raw speed
  • Practice in VelociDrone simulator
  • Learn to "punch out" (full throttle exit) from tight turns

Cinematic

🎬 What is Cinematic?

Cinematic FPV is about smooth, controlled flying that produces beautiful video footage. Think sweeping landscape reveals, building fly-throughs, and tracking shots. Used extensively in film, TV, and real estate.

Cinematic Techniques

  • Reveal shots: Fly up over a ridge to reveal a landscape
  • Orbit: Circle around a subject while keeping it in frame
  • Tracking: Follow a moving subject (car, boat, person)
  • Fly-through: Enter and exit buildings smoothly
  • Hyperlapse: Speed up footage for dramatic effect

Best Setup for Cinematic

  • Frame: 3-5 inch or Cinewhoop (ducted)
  • Video: DJI O4 Pro (records 4K onboard)
  • Camera: GoPro Hero 12 (optional, for top quality)
  • Tune: Low PIDs, high expo for smooth control
  • Weight: Heavier = more stable in wind

Long Range

🗺️ What is Long Range?

Long range FPV involves flying 10-30+ km from your position. It's about exploration — mountains, coastlines, valleys, and places you can't reach on foot.

Long Range Requirements

  • GPS: Mandatory for rescue and telemetry
  • Radio: 900MHz ELRS or Crossfire (better penetration)
  • Battery: 6S 3000mAh+ or Li-Ion pack (18650/21700)
  • Video: DJI O4 (13km+) or long-range analog
  • Range: 15-50+ km possible

Safety Rules

  • Always have a GPS module with rescue mode configured
  • Always check local laws and airspace restrictions
  • Never fly over people or populated areas
  • Always have a failsafe plan (what happens if signal lost?)
  • Monitor battery voltage — calculate return trip time
  • File a flight plan if required in your area

Tiny Whoop / Indoor

🏠 Tiny Whoop Flying

Tiny Whoops are small (65-75mm) ducted drones safe for indoor flying. They're the cheapest way into FPV and great for rainy days.

  • Frame: 65mm with prop guards
  • Battery: 1S 300-550mAh
  • Weight: 20-30g
  • Video: Analog (cheapest)
  • Cost: $80-150 complete

Indoor Flying Tips

  • Use a simulator first (FPV SkyDive is free)
  • Start in Angle mode, progress to Acro
  • Fly low, not high — ceiling is your enemy
  • Practice hovering in one spot
  • Use low rates for precise control

Learning to Fly

Phase 1: Simulator (10+ hours)

Don't skip this. Simulators save you hundreds of dollars in broken parts.

Phase 2: Tiny Whoop (5-10 hours)

  • Cheapest real-world practice
  • Safe for indoor and outdoor
  • Cheapest to repair
  • Learn orientation and throttle control

Phase 3: 5-Inch Outdoor (ongoing)

  • Start in open field, away from people
  • Fly low and slow at first
  • Gradually increase speed and altitude
  • Practice landing safely
  • Learn proximity flying

Control Inputs & Stick Rates

What are Rates?

Rates determine how fast your drone rotates at different stick positions. Higher rates = faster rotation. Most pilots use custom rates.

FPV Etiquette & Safety

  • Don't fly over people — ever
  • Respect other pilots' space at flying spots
  • Keep it under 120m/400ft in most countries
  • Maintain visual line of sight (or use a spotter)
  • Don't fly near airports — check airspace maps
  • Pick up your trash — props, zip ties, etc.
  • Share knowledge with new pilots
  • Register your drone if required in your country
