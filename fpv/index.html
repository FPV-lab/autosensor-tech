What is FPV?

FPV (First Person View) is a method of piloting a drone where the operator sees exactly what the drone's onboard camera sees in real-time, through specialized video goggles. This immersive perspective creates the sensation of sitting in the cockpit, enabling incredibly precise and dynamic control.


Unlike traditional camera drones (like DJI Mavic) which are GPS-stabilized and flown via smartphone screens, FPV drones are fully manual - the pilot has complete control over every movement. This results in a much steeper learning curve but enables capabilities impossible with GPS drones:


  • Speeds exceeding 200 km/h
  • Acrobatic maneuvers: flips, rolls, powerloops
  • Precision flying through tight gaps
  • Cinematic shots impossible with any other camera system

Safety Rules

  • Never charge unattended
  • Use LiPo safe bags
  • Storage voltage: 3.85V/cell
  • Never below 3.0V/cell
  • Always balance charge
  • Stop if battery swells

Flying Styles

Freestyle

Flips, rolls, dives, powerloops. Most popular style. YouTube: Mr. Steele, JohnnyFPV.

Racing

Through gates at 100-200km/h+. DRL and DCL professional leagues. Requires analog or HDZero.

Cinematic

Smooth, stabilized footage for films. Used by Hollywood. YouTube: Nurk FPV.

Long Range

Mountain diving, exploration. 10km+ flights. Requires GPS and large batteries.
