1. What is FPV?

First Person View - Fly like you're in the cockpit

The FPV Experience

FPV (First Person View) is a method of piloting a drone where the operator sees exactly what the drone's onboard camera sees in real-time, through specialized video goggles. This creates an incredibly immersive experience - you feel like you're sitting in the cockpit of a tiny aircraft.


Unlike traditional camera drones (DJI Mavic, etc.) which are GPS-stabilized and flown via smartphone screens, FPV drones are fully manual. The pilot has complete control over every movement, enabling capabilities impossible with GPS drones:

FPV vs Camera Drones

FeatureFPV DroneCamera Drone
ControlFull ManualGPS Stabilized
Speed200+ km/h50-70 km/h
AgilityExtremeLimited
Learning Curve10-50 hours5 minutes

3. Evolution of FPV Technology

From analog beginnings to digital revolution

2005-2010: Early Days

First FPV flights using repurposed analog security cameras. Range limited to hundreds of meters. Pilots used CRT monitors or modified video glasses. Very expensive and difficult.

2010-2013: Multirotor Revolution

Quadcopters become viable. DJI Phantom launches (2013). GoPro + quadcopter combinations. FPV racing begins in small groups. Carbon fiber frames emerge.

2014-2016: Racing Era

FPV racing explodes in popularity. DRL (Drone Racing League) founded. Betaflight firmware development. 250mm becomes standard size. FatShark goggles dominate.

2017-2019: Miniaturization

Tiny Whoops popularized. Micro drones for indoor flying. 5-inch becomes the standard. OpenTX/EdgeTX firmware. Battery technology improves (6S standard).

2019-2020: Digital FPV Revolution

DJI FPV system launches - HD video transmission. Analog vs Digital debate begins. ExpressLRS development starts. HDZero enters market.

2022-2023: War Changes Everything

Ukraine war demonstrates FPV military value. $500 drones destroy $5M tanks. Mass production begins. Counter-UAS development accelerates globally.

2024-2026: Current State

DJI O4 Pro (4K, 13km, 15ms latency). ELRS becomes industry standard. OpenIPC digital systems. AI integration begins. Laser weapons operational. $50B+ C-UAS market.

6. Flight Controllers - The Brain

The processor that makes everything work

🧠 What Does a Flight Controller Do?

The FC is the "brain" of your drone. It reads sensor data (gyroscope, accelerometer), processes pilot inputs from the receiver, runs PID control loops, and sends commands to the ESCs to control motor speeds 1000+ times per second.


Key Functions: Gyro stabilization • PID control • OSD generation • GPS processing • Failsafe management • Telemetry • LED control • Buzzer control

7. ESCs - Motor Controllers

Electronic Speed Controllers that drive the motors

⚡ How ESCs Work

ESCs receive commands from the Flight Controller (via DShot protocol) and convert battery power into precisely timed 3-phase AC signals that drive the brushless motors. They switch thousands of times per second to control motor speed.

8. Motors - The Power

Brushless motors that spin the propellers

🔄 How Brushless Motors Work

Unlike brushed motors with physical brushes, brushless motors use electromagnetic fields to rotate. The ESC creates a rotating magnetic field by sequentially energizing motor coils, causing the permanent magnets on the rotor to spin. This design is more efficient, powerful, and durable.

11. Radio Control Systems

How you control the drone

📻 ExpressLRS (ELRS) - The 2026 Standard

ExpressLRS is an open-source radio control protocol that has become the industry standard. It offers the best combination of range, latency, reliability, and cost. Available in 2.4GHz and 900MHz versions.

12. LiPo Batteries

Power source - handle with care!

🔋 LiPo Battery Basics

LiPo (Lithium Polymer) batteries provide the high power-to-weight ratio needed for FPV drones. Each cell has a nominal voltage of 3.7V, charges to 4.2V, and must never discharge below 3.0V (damage risk).

14. Betaflight Software

The firmware that runs your drone

💻 What is Betaflight?

Betaflight is the most popular open-source flight controller firmware for FPV drones. It runs PID control loops at 8kHz, manages OSD, handles failsafe, processes GPS data, and provides a powerful configuration interface via USB or Bluetooth.

Flips, rolls, dives, powerloops, split-S, trippy spins. Most popular style. Creative expression through movement. YouTube: Mr. Steele, JohnnyFPV, Stingers Stingers.

17. Tuning & Optimization

Making your drone fly perfectly

🎛️ PID Tuning Basics

PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) control is how the drone maintains stability. Tuning these values affects how the drone responds to inputs and disturbances. Start with default Betaflight PIDs and adjust based on flight characteristics.

PID Parameters

ParameterEffect
P (Proportional)Response strength
I (Integral)Steady-state correction
D (Derivative)Damping/overshoot prevention
