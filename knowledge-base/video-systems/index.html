Analog System

Still the standard for racing and budget builds. Cheapest, lightest, no lock-in.

ComponentPriceWeightTop Brands
Camera$15-403-8gCaddx Ratel, Foxeer, RunCam
VTX$15-402-5gTBS Unify, Rush Tank, HGLRC Zeus
Goggles$80-500—Skyzone SKY04X Pro (best), Eachine EV800DM (budget)

Analog Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Cheapest, lightest (great for whoops), lowest latency, no lock-in, video sharing
  • Cons: Poor image quality (like 1970s TV), static/snow, no onboard recording
  • Best for: Racing (latency matters), budget builds, Tiny Whoops

HDZero (Racing Focus)

"Analog Plus" — digital image quality with analog-like latency behavior. Fixed low latency regardless of signal strength.

SpecValue
Resolution720p@90fps or 1080p@60fps
LatencyFixed low (~25ms worst case)
BehaviorFreezes/snow when signal lost (like analog)
Weight3-9g (multiple VTX sizes)
Lock-inHDZero goggles also support Analog!

Best for: Competitive racing where every millisecond matters.

Walksnail (DJI Alternative)

Good image quality, reasonable latency, and some goggle flexibility.

SpecValue
Resolution1080p recording + 1080p live
Range4km+ typical
Weight7-28g (Nano to Pro)
Price~$100-200 (air unit + goggles)

OpenIPC (Open Source)

The newcomer — fully open-source digital FPV. No lock-in, cheap hardware, but still maturing.

SpecValue
Resolution720-1080p
Latency~25ms
HardwareWiFi chips (ESP32, etc.)
Price$30-80 (air unit)
StatusEarly — not fully stable

Best for: Hobbyists who like tinkering and supporting open source.
