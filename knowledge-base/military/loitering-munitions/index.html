Loitering Munitions & Swarm Drones

Loitering munitions bridge the gap between cruise missiles and reconnaissance drones. They fly to an area, loiter while searching for targets, then strike. Combined with swarm technology, they represent the future of autonomous warfare.

What Are Loitering Munitions?

Loitering munitions (LMs) are weapons that can fly to a target area, circle while the operator searches for targets, and then dive onto the target. Unlike missiles (fire-and-forget), LMs give the operator time to identify and select targets. Unlike FPV drones (piloted manually), LMs can operate semi-autonomously.

Key Characteristics

  • Can loiter for 15-60+ minutes over target area
  • Operator selects targets via camera feed
  • Some models have autonomous target recognition
  • Can abort mission and return if no target found (recoverable models)
  • Larger payload and range than FPV drones
  • Significantly more expensive than improvised FPV drones

Swarm Drones

Swarm technology coordinates multiple drones to operate as a unified system. Instead of one drone attacking a target, dozens or hundreds work together autonomously.

Swarm Concepts

  • Saturation Attack: Overwhelm air defenses with numbers — if 50 drones attack simultaneously, defenses can only engage a fraction
  • Distributed Sensing: Multiple drones share intelligence, building a complete picture of the battlespace
  • Autonomous Coordination: Drones communicate peer-to-peer, assigning targets without human intervention
  • Resilience: Lose half the swarm and the rest adapts — no single point of failure

Bomber Drones

A newer category: modified commercial or custom drones carrying mortar rounds, grenades, or small bombs for precision dropping.

  • Typically use release mechanisms to drop small munitions from altitude
  • More precise than artillery but slower and limited payload
  • Used extensively in Ukraine for trench warfare
  • Carry VOG-17 grenades, mortar rounds, or custom munitions
  • Some systems use AI-assisted targeting for accuracy
