📻 Radio Control

Transmitters, receivers, and the protocols that connect you to your drone

How Radio Control Works

Your radio transmitter sends control signals to a receiver on the drone. The receiver passes commands to the flight controller. In 2026, ExpressLRS (ELRS) has become the dominant protocol, offering the best combination of range, speed, and price.

2026 Standard: EdgeTX firmware + ExpressLRS protocol + 2.4GHz band. This combination offers 1000Hz update rate, 10km+ range, and costs under $100 for the transmitter.

ExpressLRS (ELRS) Deep Dive

ExpressLRS is an open-source radio control protocol that has revolutionized FPV. It offers the best performance at the lowest cost, which is why it's become the industry standard.

ELRS Setup

  1. Flash ELRS firmware to TX module using flasher.expresslrs.org
  2. Flash ELRS firmware to RX with same binding phrase
  3. Set packet rate (250Hz recommended for range, 500Hz for racing)
  4. Bind: power cycle RX 3 times within 30 seconds of powering TX
  5. In Betaflight: set receiver to CRSF protocol on the correct UART

Firmware: EdgeTX

EdgeTX is the open-source firmware that runs on most modern transmitters. It's a fork of OpenTX with more features and active development.

EdgeTX Features

  • Custom mixes and curves for precise control
  • Logical switches and special functions
  • Model profiles (up to 60+ models)
  • Telemetry display (battery voltage, RSSI, GPS)
  • Multi-protocol support
  • Color touchscreen UI (on supported radios)

Antenna Placement

  • Mount RX antenna away from carbon fiber (blocks RF)
  • Use zip ties to keep antenna rigid and exposed
  • For diversity: mount antennas 90° apart
  • Keep antenna away from VTX antenna (interference)
  • T-antenna: mount vertically for best omnidirectional coverage
