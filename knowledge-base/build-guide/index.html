Assembly Steps

Step 1: Frame Assembly

Assemble the frame bottom plate with arms. Tighten all screws but don't over-tighten carbon fiber (it can crack).

  • Identify front, back, left, right arms
  • Stack bottom plate + arms + hardware
  • Leave standoffs for FC/ESC stack
Step 2: Install Motors

Mount all 4 motors to the arms. Pay attention to motor direction (2 clockwise, 2 counter-clockwise).

  • Use M3 screws (usually 6-8mm length)
  • Motor wires should face toward center
  • Tighten screws firmly but not excessively
  • Label motors: front-left (CCW), front-right (CW), back-left (CW), back-right (CCW)
Step 3: Solder ESC Power Wires

Solder the XT60 pigtail (battery connector wire) to the ESC power pads.

  • Red wire → + (positive) pad
  • Black wire → - (negative) pad
  • Use plenty of flux
  • Heat pad first, then add solder
  • Add capacitor (1000µF 35V) across power pads
⚠️ Triple-check polarity! Reversed polarity will destroy your ESC instantly. Red goes to +, black to -. No exceptions.
Step 4: Connect Motors to ESC

Solder the 3 motor wires to the corresponding ESC motor pads. Wire order determines direction.

  • Use 3 motor wires per motor (any order for now)
  • Solder to ESC M1-M4 motor pads
  • Keep wires short but with enough slack
  • Motor direction will be fixed in BLHeli later
Step 5: Stack FC on ESC

Connect the flight controller on top of the 4-in-1 ESC using the included connector.

  • Align the connector pins correctly
  • Use rubber standoffs/grommets for vibration dampening
  • The FC arrow should point toward the FRONT of the drone

Step 8: First Power-On Test

Before closing everything up, do a power-on test.

  • Use a smoke stopper if you have one
  • Connect USB first → verify FC recognized by Betaflight Configurator
  • Disconnect USB, connect battery briefly
  • Check for heat, smoke, or odd smells
  • Verify DJI O4 link to goggles
  • Bind ELRS receiver to transmitter
⚠️ Use a smoke stopper! A $15 smoke stopper can save a $400 build. It limits current and prevents shorts from destroying components.
Step 9: Betaflight Configuration

Configure all settings in Betaflight Configurator. See the Software page for details.

  • Ports: Enable Serial RX on UART 1, MSP+DisplayPort on UART 2
  • Receiver: CRSF protocol
  • Motors: Set motor direction (props out or props in)
  • Arming: Set AUX1 switch
  • Failsafe: GPS Rescue or Drop
  • OSD: Enable voltage, timer, LQ, throttle
Step 10: Motor Direction & Propellers

Verify motor direction. Use the Betaflight motor tab to check each motor.

  • Props-out (default): Front motors spin outward, rear motors spin outward
  • Props-in: Opposite direction
  • If direction is wrong: use BLHeli Suite or ESC Configurator to reverse
  • Install propellers matching motor direction (check prop A/B markings)
Step 11: Final Assembly

Close up the frame, secure all wires, and do final checks.

  • Tuck all wires inside frame, away from props
  • Secure battery connector at bottom
  • Tighten all frame screws
  • Verify antenna placement
  • Add battery strap
  • Test all switches and failsafe

Common Mistakes

  • Reversed polarity: Double and triple check +/- on all power connections
  • Cold solder joints: Heat the pad, not the solder. Shiny = good, dull = bad
  • Props on during testing: Never have props on during motor direction tests
  • Short FC arrow: The arrow on the FC points FORWARD. Get this wrong and your drone flies backward
  • Loose antenna: A loose antenna = short range and failsafes
  • No capacitor: Always add a capacitor on the power lead to reduce electrical noise
