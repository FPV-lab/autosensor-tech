Learning to Fly

Learning FPV is like learning to ride a bike — the first hours are frustrating, then suddenly everything clicks. This guide maps the path from complete beginner to confident pilot. Spoiler: simulators save you hundreds of dollars in crashes.

Flight Modes Explained

Betaflight offers three main flight modes. Understanding them is crucial for learning progression.

ModeBehaviorUse Case
ANGLE (Self-Level)Quad auto-levels when sticks are centered. Max tilt angle limited (default 45°). Sticks control angle, not rotation rate.Beginners, first flights, GPS rescue mode
HORIZONSelf-levels at low stick inputs, allows flips/rolls at full stick. Hybrid between Angle and Acro.Transition mode between Angle and Acro
ACRO (Rate)Sticks control rotation rate. Quad holds whatever angle you leave it at. No auto-level. Full 3D control.All FPV flying once learned. This is the mode.

Start in Acro

Most FPV instructors recommend starting in Acro mode from day one — on a simulator. Angle mode builds bad habits (relying on auto-level). Acro is harder initially but builds proper muscle memory that transfers to real flying.

Connect Your Radio

All sims support USB connection from your radio (Radiomaster, Jumper, etc.). This is crucial — sim flying must feel identical to real flying. Configure the sim to match your radio's channel order (AETR) and rates.

Learning Phases

Phase 1: Simulator (2-10 hours)

  • Learn to hover, pitch, roll, and yaw in Acro mode
  • Practice basic turns around a course
  • Goal: Fly a complete lap without crashing
  • Goal: Smooth turns maintaining altitude

Phase 2: Tiny Whoop / Micro (5-20 flights)

  • Indoor micro drones (65-75mm) — crash-safe, cheap to repair
  • Apply sim skills to real hardware
  • Learn throttle control in real gravity
  • Practice in your house — navigate rooms, doorways
  • Budget: BetaFPV Cetus Pro ($80-120 all-in-one kit)

Phase 3: 5-Inch Outdoors (20-50+ flights)

  • First flights in wide open field — no obstacles
  • Start with hover and basic orientation
  • Gradually add speed, turns, altitude changes
  • Practice in Angle mode first if nervous, switch to Acro ASAP
  • Expect crashes — they happen to everyone

Phase 4: Progression (ongoing)

  • Explore different flying styles (freestyle, racing, cinematic)
  • Try new spots and environments
  • Record and review your flights — Blackbox and GoPro footage
  • Join local FPV groups and fly with others

Stick Rates

Rates control how fast your quad responds to stick input. Start slow, increase as your skills improve.

FPV Etiquette & Safety

⚠️ Safety Rules — Always Follow

  • Never fly over people — Drones can fall, fail, and hurt someone
  • Stay below 120m / 400ft AGL (check local limits)
  • Keep visual line of sight or use a spotter
  • Register your drone if required by local laws
  • Don't fly near airports — check NOTAMs and restricted zones
  • Respect private property — ask permission before flying
  • Be aware of other aircraft — manned aircraft always have right of way
  • Fly sober — no alcohol or drugs while operating

Community Etiquette

  • Share spots responsibly — don't blow up secret locations on social media
  • Help newcomers — everyone was a beginner once
  • Don't fly over others' races or filming sessions
  • Pick up your trash — leave spots cleaner than you found them
  • Respect wildlife — don't chase birds or disturb animals
