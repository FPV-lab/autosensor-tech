Freestyle Flying

Freestyle is the soul of FPV. It's about creative expression through flight — flowing through landscapes, performing tricks, and finding lines that feel impossible. There are no rules, no gates, no judges. Just you, your quad, and whatever environment you choose to shred.

What is Freestyle?

Freestyle FPV combines proximity flying with acrobatic maneuvers. Pilots fly through gaps, around objects, over buildings, and through forests — all while performing rolls, flips, dives, and power loops. The goal is to create visually stunning, fluid flights that feel like a first-person action movie.

What Makes Freestyle Special

  • No course or gates — you create your own path
  • Combines technical skill with creative vision
  • Can be done almost anywhere (with permission)
  • Emphasis on smooth, connected lines rather than raw speed
  • The video is the art — flying is the brushstroke

Freestyle Tips

Learn the Fundamentals First

  • Master hovering — If you can't hold position smoothly, you're not ready for tricks
  • Learn to fly backwards — Many maneuvers involve reversing direction
  • Practice throttle control — Altitude management is everything in freestyle
  • Use your environment — Trees, buildings, terrain features make lines interesting

Building Smooth Lines

  • Think ahead — Always know your next 2-3 maneuvers before doing them
  • Connect tricks — A power loop into a roll into a gap shot flows; random tricks don't
  • Vary altitude — Mix high and low, dives and climbs
  • Respect gravity — Use dives to build speed, climbs to bleed it off
  • Find a spot — Returning to the same location lets you learn its potential

Camera Angle

Camera Tilt for Freestyle

Most freestyle pilots fly at 20-35° camera angle. Lower angles (15-20°) are easier to learn but limit speed. Higher angles (35°+) are faster and more aggressive. Start at 25° and adjust based on feel. Remember: camera angle affects how you perceive the horizon — more tilt = more "looking down."

Safety & Etiquette

⚠️ Freestyle Safety Rules

  • Never fly over people or populated areas
  • Get permission for private property spots
  • Check local regulations — many places restrict FPV flying
  • Always maintain visual line of sight or use a spotter
  • Don't fly near airports, helipads, or emergency operations
  • Respect wildlife — don't disturb birds or animals
  • Clean up after yourself — leave spots better than you found them
