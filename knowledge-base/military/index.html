⚔️ Military Applications

How FPV drones are reshaping modern warfare

Disclaimer: This page is for educational and research purposes. We document the technological evolution of military drone use based on publicly available information. AutoSensor Tech does not manufacture, sell, or develop weapons systems.

The Paradigm Shift

The Russia-Ukraine conflict (2022-present) has fundamentally changed military thinking about drones. A $500 FPV drone can destroy a $5 million tank. This asymmetric cost advantage has made small drones the most significant military technology development since the invention of the IED.

Types of Military Drones

1. Attack/Kamikaze FPV Drones

The most common type. Small FPV drones carrying 1-3 kg of explosive payload, flown directly into targets by an operator wearing FPV goggles.

  • Cost: $300-800 per unit
  • Speed: 100-180 km/h
  • Range: 5-20 km
  • Payload: RPG-7 warhead, RKG-3, improvised charges
  • Effectiveness: 50-80% hit rate with trained pilots

2. Reconnaissance Drones

Used for real-time intelligence, artillery correction, and battle damage assessment.

  • Examples: DJI Mavic 3, custom fixed-wing
  • Purpose: Real-time video feed to command
  • Range: 10-50 km
  • Endurance: 30-90 minutes

3. Loitering Munitions

Larger than FPV, these are purpose-built weapons that can loiter over an area waiting for targets.

4. Swarm Drones

Multiple drones operating in coordination to overwhelm air defenses.

  • Concept: 10-1000+ drones attack simultaneously
  • Challenge: Coordination, communication, jamming resistance
  • Status: Early stage, China and US leading development
  • Application: Saturate enemy air defense systems

5. First-Person-View (FPV) Bomber Drones

Larger FPV drones carrying multiple small munitions, dropping grenades on targets below.

  • Payload: 2-5 kg, multiple drops
  • Advantage: Reusable (if not shot down)
  • Use: Infantry trenches, soft targets

Key Conflicts & Deployments

🇺🇦🇷🇺 Russia-Ukraine War (2022-Present)

The defining drone war. Both sides use drones extensively:

  • Ukraine produces 100,000+ FPV drones per month
  • Russia deploys Shahed-136 in massive nightly waves
  • Drones account for 60-70% of vehicle kills in some sectors
  • "Drone walls" along front lines make movement deadly
  • Electronic warfare (jamming) is a major countermeasure
  • Fiber-optic guided drones emerging to counter jamming

Other Conflicts

  • Nagorno-Karabakh (2020): Azerbaijan used TB2 Bayraktar and Israeli loitering munitions to devastating effect
  • Syria/Libya: Turkish TB2 proven effective against conventional forces
  • Houthi attacks: Cheap drones attacking Saudi oil infrastructure
  • Red Sea: Houthi drone/missile attacks on shipping

Technology Trends (2025-2030)

  • AI Autonomy: Drones that identify and engage targets without human control (controversial)
  • Fiber-Optic Guidance: Wire-guided drones immune to jamming (5-20 km range)
  • Mesh Networking: Drones communicating and coordinating autonomously
  • Counter-Countermeasures: Frequency hopping, spread spectrum, AI-driven EW resistance
  • Swarm Intelligence: Hundreds of drones acting as a single organism
  • Underwater Drones: USVs and UUVs expanding the battlespace
  • Directed Energy Weapons: Lasers and high-power microwaves for drone defense

Ethical Considerations

  • Autonomous weapons: Should machines decide who lives and dies?
  • Proliferation: Small drones are easy to manufacture and hard to control
  • International law: Existing treaties struggle to address drones
  • Civilian impact: Drones lower the barrier to warfare
  • Dual-use: The same tech used for agriculture can be weaponized
