Cinematic & Long Range

Cinematic FPV creates breathtaking aerial footage that was previously impossible — flying through windows, chasing cars, diving down cliff faces. Long range pushes the boundaries of what's possible with extended flight time and radio/video range.

Cinematic Techniques

Cinematic flying is about intentional, planned shots. Unlike freestyle, every movement serves the story. Slow, smooth, deliberate.

TechniqueDescriptionBest Use
The RevealRise up over an obstacle to reveal a landscape or subject. Starts hidden, ends spectacular.Mountain tops, city skylines, ocean views
OrbitCircle around a subject at constant distance and altitude. Smooth, continuous motion.Buildings, monuments, events, people
Tracking ShotFollow a moving subject (car, person, boat) at matched speed.Action sequences, sports, vehicles
Fly-ThroughFly through windows, doorways, arches, or narrow spaces. Extreme proximity.Architecture, abandoned buildings, forests
Top-DownCamera pointing straight down, creating a flat map-like perspective.Patterns, landscapes, shadows
Nose-Down DiveDive from altitude at steep angle, pulling up at last moment.Cliffs, waterfalls, tall structures
Parallax MoveMove laterally while keeping subject centered, foreground blurs past.Trees, fences, corridors
DronieFly backward and up from a subject, revealing more of the scene.Introductions, establishing shots

The 180° Shutter Rule

For natural motion blur, set shutter speed to double your frame rate. At 30fps → 1/60s shutter. At 60fps → 1/120s shutter. Since FPV cameras are bright outdoors, use ND filters to achieve these slow shutter speeds without overexposing.

Long Range FPV

Long range flying means pushing beyond visual line of sight — exploring mountains, coastlines, valleys. It requires careful planning, redundant systems, and respect for the limits of your equipment.

Long Range Safety Rules

⚠️ Long Range Safety — Non-Negotiable

  • Always use GPS Rescue — Test it before flying long range
  • Never fly beyond radio/video range — Check RSSI and LQ continuously
  • Plan your flight path — Know where you'll be at all times
  • Tell someone where you're flying — Leave a flight plan
  • Check weather forecast — Wind, rain, temperature all matter
  • Know your battery limits — Set voltage alarms, land with margin
  • Carry spare batteries and tools — You're remote, stores are far
  • Respect airspace regulations — Check NOTAMs, altitude limits, restricted zones
